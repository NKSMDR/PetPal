Nutrition & Diet

Feeding guidelines for your {{ breed.name }}

Dietary Recommendations

The {{ breed.name }} thrives on a high-quality, balanced diet appropriate for their size, age, and activity level. Choose dog food that lists real meat as the first ingredient and is specifically formulated for {{ breed.size|lower }} breeds.

Protein: Essential for muscle development and maintenance. Look for 25-30% protein content in their food.
Healthy Fats: Omega-3 and Omega-6 fatty acids support coat health and cognitive function.
Fiber & Carbs: Provide energy and aid digestion. Whole grains, sweet potatoes, and vegetables are excellent sources.
Vitamins & Minerals: Support immune system, bone health, and overall wellbeing.

Feeding Schedule

Puppies (2-12 months)

3-4 meals per day

Adults (1-7 years)

2 meals per day

Seniors (7+ years)

2 meals per day (senior formula)

Always provide fresh water and avoid feeding table scraps or toxic foods like chocolate, grapes, and onions.

Health & Wellness

Keeping your {{ breed.name }} healthy and happy

Common Health Considerations

While {{ breed.name }}s are generally healthy dogs, being aware of potential health issues helps with early detection and prevention:

    {% if breed.size == "Large" %}
  • Hip Dysplasia: Common in larger breeds. Maintain healthy weight and provide joint supplements.
  • Bloat: Use slow-feed bowls and avoid exercise immediately after meals.
    • {% elif breed.size == "Small" %}
  • Dental Issues: Small breeds are prone to dental problems. Regular teeth cleaning is essential.
  • Patellar Luxation: Knee cap issues can occur. Monitor for limping or difficulty jumping.
    • {% endif %}
  • Regular Checkups: Annual vet visits for early detection of potential issues.
  • Vaccinations: Keep up-to-date with core vaccines and preventive medications.
  • Weight Management: Obesity can lead to various health problems. Monitor food intake and exercise.

Preventive Care Tips

Vaccinations

Core vaccines (rabies, distemper, parvovirus) and optional vaccines based on lifestyle.

Parasite Prevention

Monthly flea, tick, and heartworm prevention year-round for optimal protection.

Weight Monitoring

Regular weigh-ins and body condition assessments to maintain ideal weight.

Exercise & Mental Stimulation

Regular physical activity and puzzle toys to keep body and mind healthy.

Training & Socialization

Building a well-behaved and confident {{ breed.name }}

Early Socialization

Expose your {{ breed.name }} puppy to various people, animals, environments, and experiences during their critical socialization period (3-14 weeks). This builds confidence and prevents fearfulness.

Positive Reinforcement

Use rewards-based training with treats, praise, and play. {{ breed.name }}s respond best to positive methods rather than punishment or harsh corrections.

Consistency is Key

Establish clear rules and boundaries from day one. All family members should use the same commands and reward good behavior consistently.
